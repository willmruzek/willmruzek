## Designing a new blog theme in public: a real‑world post that exercises everything

When I set out to refresh my blog’s theme, I needed a realistic post to pressure‑test typography, spacing, and components. This is that post: a narrative walkthrough that just happens to use every bit of Markdown (and a little MDX) you’ll run into in day‑to‑day writing.[^1]

## Why a realistic test post?

Good design starts with real content. Placeholder paragraphs don’t capture the nuance of emphasis, quotes, tables, images, or code. So let’s build a small story around refining this site.

> “Design systems are only as good as the content they’re designed for.”
>
> I kept this in mind while iterating on the type scale, line lengths, and spacing.

### Small decisions add up

This paragraph lives under an H3 to validate hierarchy. It helps ensure the jump from H2 → H3 feels intentional, with adequate contrast and spacing.

#### Micro‑typography matters

Kerning, line‑height, and rhythm often decide whether a page feels polished. Even tiny headings deserve attention.

##### Don’t forget the edge cases

Fifth‑level headings appear in longer posts—think deep dives or API docs—so they should still be readable with _italics_ and **bold** where needed.

###### The tiniest heading still needs care

At H6 size, headings can collapse into body text. Confirm they remain distinct on small screens.

## Content typography checks

Inline emphasis shows up all the time: _italics_ for tone, **bold** for weight, and even ~~strikethrough~~ to acknowledge a change of mind. Inline `code` marks identifiers like `Button` or `process.env.NODE_ENV`. Reference links like [Vercel][vercel] keep text clean, while an autolink like https://github.com is handy in notes. Abbreviations (<abbr title="HyperText Markup Language">HTML</abbr>), keyboard keys (<kbd>Cmd</kbd> + <kbd>K</kbd>), and even a touch of math 10<sup>2</sup> or chemistry H<sub>2</sub>O appear naturally. Sometimes you just need to <mark>highlight a phrase</mark>.

> A quick aside in a quote helps establish voice and pacing.
>
> > And a nested quote confirms spacing doesn’t collapse.
